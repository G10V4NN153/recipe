HOW TO MAKE MOLE

Here is a brief history of the Mole in Mexico. Mole has become a plate which has made Mexico different and represents it in gastronomic terms.The name comes from the the Nahualli term "molli" and it refers to sauces prepared from a mix of chilis and spices. It dates back to the prehispanic era but has been transformed over the time. The origens of the version we know today are unknown but the most known theory is that it was created by the nun Andrea de la Asuncion Puebla City for the Viceroy Tomas Antonio de Serna when he was in the city.There are over 50 different types of mole but the states that stand out when preparing mole are Guerrero, Oaxaca , Puebla and Veracruz.

    Ingredients

    For the chicken

  • 4 chicken thighs and legs
  • 1/4 of white onion
  • garlic clove
  • 1 chopped celery stick
  • One small sprig of Parsley
  • Enough water to cook the chicken

    • For the mole sauce

  • 4 pasilla chillis cleaned and deveined
  • 2 plum tomatoes
  • 1/4 of white onion
  • Garlic clove
  • 1 cup of chicken broth
  • 1 spoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 tablet of mexican chocoalate
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Place the chicken, onion and garlic clove in a pot and cover them with water. Boil and cook for 5-6 minutes on medium heat and removing any layer of foam witha spoon. Reduce the flame after the 5 minutes and add the pieces of celery and the parsley, continue cooking for aproximately 20-25 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile the chicken is cooking lightly toast the chilis in a hot griddle(comal) and rotate them to avoid burning them and having a bitter flavor. Do this for a couple of seconds and then remove the chilis and place them in a container with warm water to soak them for 15 minutes(until they are soft).
  3. Add a layer of non stic spray to the comal and place the tomato the onion and garlic to roast, dont forget to rotate. Do this until they have brown spots from the grill.
  4. Take the pasilla chilis out and put them in a blender with the grilled onions, grilled tomatoes grilled garlic and the cup of chicken broth. Blend until the it becomes a sauce.
  5. Heat a spoon of oil in a big pan to a medium high heat then place and suatee the sauce and stirr to avoid making it stick.
  6. Remove the chicken from the pot so it lightly cools down and throw away the parsley, onion, garlic clove and celery that was with the chicken. Add the chicken to the sauce on low heat and season pepper amd salt.
  7. You can serve this dish with white rice and torillas
